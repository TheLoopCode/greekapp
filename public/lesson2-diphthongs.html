Lesson 2: Diphthongs & Special Combinations

Learn the most important Greek vowel pairs and consonant combinations, then practice with flashcards.

Greek diphthongs are vowel pairs that produce a single sound, like αι, ει, οι, and ου. Some letters change their sound depending on what follows, and a few combinations behave like special consonants.

Note: αι and ει usually sound like /eh/ and /ee/, while οι and ου sound like /ee/ and /oo/. Exceptions happen in modern Greek when ευ and αυ switch between /ev/ or /ef/ and /av/ or /af/ depending on the next consonant.
